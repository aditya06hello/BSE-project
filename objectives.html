Evaluating the Effectiveness of AI-Assisted Simulations in Nursing Education

Objective

The aim of this study was to evaluate the effectiveness of artificial intelligence-assisted screen-based simulations practice and standard patient simulation in teaching breast self-examination skills in nursing undergraduate students.

Detailed Explanation

  1. The aim of this study:

    This phrase introduces the primary goal or purpose of the research.

  2. was to evaluate the effectiveness:

    The study intends to assess how well something works or how beneficial it is.

  3. of artificial intelligence-assisted screen-based simulations practice:
    • Artificial Intelligence-assisted: This indicates the use of AI technology to enhance the learning experience.
    • Screen-based simulations practice: This refers to training sessions conducted on digital platforms, where scenarios are simulated on a screen. These simulations are interactive and designed to mimic real-life situations.
  4. and standard patient simulation:
    • Standard patient simulation: This involves the use of actors or mannequins to simulate real patients. It is a hands-on approach where students practice on life-like models or with people acting as patients.
  5. in teaching breast self-examination skills:

    The specific skill being taught and evaluated in the study is how to perform a breast self-examination. This is a crucial skill for early detection of breast abnormalities, including potential signs of breast cancer.

  6. in nursing undergraduate students:

    The target group for this study is students who are pursuing an undergraduate degree in nursing. These students are being trained in essential healthcare skills as part of their curriculum.

Summary

The study aims to compare and measure the effectiveness of two different teaching methods—AI-assisted digital simulations and traditional patient simulations—in teaching nursing students how to perform breast self-examinations. The objective is to determine which method better prepares students in acquiring this critical skill.
